Knowledge Graphs

A Knowledge Graph is a way of representing information using entities and relationships between them.

Instead of storing information separately, related concepts are connected together in the form of a graph. This makes searching and reasoning easier.

For example:
- Person -> lives in -> City
- City -> located in -> Country

Knowledge Graphs are commonly used in:
- search engines
- recommendation systems
- question answering systems

Some tools used for building Knowledge Graphs are:
- Protégé
- Neo4j
- RDF
- OWL

Knowledge Graphs are useful because they organize data in a more connected and meaningful way instead of storing everything independently.
